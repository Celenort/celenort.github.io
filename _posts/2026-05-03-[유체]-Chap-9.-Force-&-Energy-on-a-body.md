# Chap 9. Force and Energy on a body


## 9.1 Force on a circular cylinder in a uniform flow


{% raw %}
$$
\vec{F}=\int p\vec{n}\,ds
$$
{% endraw %}



$p$ : pressure


{% raw %}
$$
\vec{M}=\int \rho(\vec{r}\times\vec{n})\,ds
$$
{% endraw %}



(Momentum)


From Bernoulli equation,


{% raw %}
$$
\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}+\frac{1}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2+gz+\frac{P}{\rho}=C(t)
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\vec{F}=-\rho\int\left\{\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}+\frac{1}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2+gz+C(t)\right\}\vec{n}\,ds
$$
{% endraw %}



Ignore hydrostatic pressure $-\int \rho gz\vec{n}\,ds$


Ignore constant term $-\int C(t)\vec{n}\,ds=0$ for closed body (by divergence theorem)


Steady flow $\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}=0$


{% raw %}
$$
\therefore\vec{F}=-\int_{S_B}\frac{\rho}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2\vec{n}\,ds
$$
{% endraw %}



## 9.2 Potential flow around the circular cylinder


{% raw %}
$$
\phi=U\cos\theta\left(r+\frac{a^2}{r}\right)=Ux+U\frac{a^2x}{r^2}
$$
{% endraw %}



$a$ : radius of cylinder


{% raw %}
$$
\nabla\phi=\left\{\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial r},\frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial\theta}\right\}=\{0,-2U\sin\theta\}
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\vec{F}=\int-\rho\left(\frac{1}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2\right)\vec{n}\,ds
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
=-\rho\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{1}{2}(2U\sin\theta)^2a\,d\theta\,\vec{n}
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
=-\rho\int_0^{2\pi}2U^2\sin^2\theta\,a(-\cos\theta,-\sin\theta)\,d\theta=-(0,0)=\vec{0}
$$
{% endraw %}



⇒ D’Alembert’s Paradox


### Uniform + 2D dipole + 2D vortex


![](/assets/img/2026-05-03-[유체]-Chap-9.-Force-&-Energy-on-a-body/0-396c6aed01.png)


{% raw %}
$$
\phi=U\cos\theta\left(r+\frac{R^2}{r}\right)+\frac{\Gamma}{2\pi}\theta
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\nabla\phi\big|_{r=a}=\left(0,2U\sin\theta+\frac{\Gamma}{2\pi a}\right)
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\vec{F}=\int_0^{2\pi}-\frac{\rho}{2}\left(4U^2\sin^2\theta-\frac{2U\Gamma}{\pi a}\sin\theta+\frac{\Gamma^2}{4\pi^2a^2}\right)(-\cos\theta,-\sin\theta)a\,d\theta
$$
{% endraw %}



$F_x = 0$, 


{% raw %}
$$
F_y=\int_0^{2\pi}-\frac{\rho}{2}\frac{2U\Gamma}{\pi a}\sin^2\theta\,a\,d\theta = -\rho U \Gamma \text{ (Lift) }
$$
{% endraw %}



⇒ Kutta- Joukowski theorem


{% raw %}
$$
\text{2D:}\quad F_y=-\rho U\Gamma
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\text{3D:}\quad \vec{F}_y=\rho\vec{U}\times\vec{\Gamma}
$$
{% endraw %}



In general form,


{% raw %}
$$
\vec{F}=\rho\vec{U}\times\sum\vec{\Gamma}
$$
{% endraw %}



## 9.3 Translation of a sphere in infinite flow

- Called BVP (Boundary value problem)
1. $\nabla^2\phi=0$ (Laplace Eqn)
2. $\phi=0\quad\text{as}\quad r\to\infty$ for moving body
3. $\vec{u}\cdot\vec{n}=U\cos\theta$ : Body force (no penetration)

Instantaneous Potential


{% raw %}
$$
\phi=-\frac{UR^3}{2r^2}\cos\theta
$$
{% endraw %}



Kinetic Energy


{% raw %}
$$
\text{K.E.}=\frac{1}{2}\rho\int_{S_B} \phi \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial n}ds
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\begin{aligned}\text{K.E.}
&=
\frac{1}{2}\rho
\iiint_{C.V.} |\nabla\phi|^2\,d\forall \\ &=
\frac{1}{2}\rho
\iiint
\nabla\cdot(\phi\nabla\phi)
-
\phi\nabla^2\phi\,dV \\ &=
\frac{1}{2}\rho
\iint
\phi\nabla\phi\cdot\vec{n}\,ds
\\ &=
\frac{1}{2}\rho
\iint
\phi\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial n}\,ds\end{aligned}
$$
{% endraw %}



Recall : $\phi=-\frac{UR^3}{2r^2}\cos\theta$


{% raw %}
$$
\begin{aligned}\text{K.E.}
&=
\frac{1}{2}\rho
\int
\left(
-\frac{UR^3}{2r^2}\cos\theta
\right)
\left(
\frac{UR^3}{r^3}
\right)
(-\cos\theta)\,ds \\ &=
\rho\int
\left(
\frac{UR}{2r}
\right)
\left(
\frac{UR^3}{r^3}
\right)
\cos^2\theta\,ds \bigg|_{r=R}\\ &=
\frac{\rho}{4}U^2R
\int\cos^2\theta\,ds\end{aligned}
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\begin{aligned}\text{K.E.}
&=
\frac{\rho U^2R}{2}
\int \cos^2\theta\cdot2\pi R^2\sin\theta\,d\theta \\ &=
\frac{\rho U^2R^3\pi}{2}
\int_0^\pi\cos^2\theta\sin\theta\,d\theta \\ &=
\frac{1}{2}U^2
\left(
\rho\cdot
\frac{1}{3}\cos^3\theta\bigg|_0^\pi
\right)
R^3\pi \\ &=
\frac{1}{2}U^2\rho
\left(
\frac{2}{3}\pi R^3
\right)\end{aligned}
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
V=\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3, m=\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3\rho
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
\text{K.E.} =
\frac{1}{2}U^2\left(\frac{1}{2}m\right) = \frac{1}{2} m_a U^2
$$
{% endraw %}



Total Energy


{% raw %}
$$
\mathrm{Total\ E}
=
\frac{1}{2}(M+m_a)U^2
$$
{% endraw %}



in 3D : $m_a = \frac{1}{2}m$,  2D : $M$


### Power to move the body

- Another perspective to approach on D’Alembert’s Paradox
- Power : Rate of Kinematic Energy increase

{% raw %}
$$
P=\frac{d}{dt}\left[K.E._{\mathrm{total}}\right]=(M+m_a)U\frac{dU}{dt}+\frac{1}{2}U^2\frac{d}{dt}(M+m_a)
$$
{% endraw %}



Constant speed : $\frac{dU}{dt}=0$


Infinite fluid : $\frac{d}{dt}(M+m_a)=0$


$P=0, FU=0, U \neq 0 \rightarrow F=0$


to move an object at constant velocity


⇒ D’Alembert’s paradox


{% raw %}
$$
(M+m_a)U\frac{dU}{dt}=FU
$$
{% endraw %}



{% raw %}
$$
F=(M+m_a)\frac{dU}{dt}
$$
{% endraw %}
